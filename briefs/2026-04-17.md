# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 17, 2026

*Ticketmaster is literally a criminal, your 401(k) is near all-time highs, and Netflix just had a rough Thursday night.*

THE OPEN

Markets finished the week green and quiet — like that one guy at the party who didn't drink but still had a good time. Nasdaq's on a 12-game winning streak. We take it.

BIG THREE

- The ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon is the real reason your 401(k) looks good right now. When Middle East tensions flare up, oil gets expensive, gas gets expensive, everything gets expensive, and stocks freak out. When things cool down, the whole machine exhales. We fell almost 10% in late March. We've clawed back more than that since. One diplomatic phone call is doing more for your retirement account than anything on CNBC.

- Netflix dropped 10% after hours and honestly it's a little embarrassing. Revenue up 16%, $12 billion in the door — that's a great quarter. But the guy who founded the company is leaving in June, and their forecast for next quarter underwhelmed some investors. Wall Street is the friend who gets you a great birthday gift and then gets mad you didn't thank them fast enough. The stock will probably be fine. But tonight it's eating dinner alone.

- TSMC — the Taiwanese company that makes the actual chips that power all your AI stuff — crushed earnings. Net income up 58% year over year. That matters because TSMC is basically the factory for every AI ambition on earth. If they're doing well, the AI wave is still real and not just vibes. Good sign.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

The retail investor pile-in is worth knowing about. "Retail investors" just means regular people — us. Not hedge funds, not Goldman Sachs. Just guys with a Robinhood account or a 401(k) they check on their phone. According to JPMorgan, regular people largely sat out this recent rally, watching from the sidelines. Now that the market's near record highs, they're jumping back in. That's historically when you want to be careful — not because you should sell everything, but because when everyone finally decides it's safe to pile in near the top, there's less room to run and more room to fall. Not a prediction. Just something to file away.

KEEP AN EYE ON

- Ticketmaster is now legally a monopoly, per a federal jury. Thirty-four states sued them. They lost. What happens next — whether they actually get broken up or just pay a fine and keep charging you $40 in fees to see a band you kind of like — that plays out over the next few months.

- Oil sitting near $92 a barrel means the Fed isn't cutting interest rates anytime soon. That means mortgage rates stay high. That means car loans stay expensive. The ceasefire is a start. Oil needs to come way down before any of that changes.

THE CLOSE

It's Friday. Your 401(k) had a good week. Netflix did not. Pour one for Reed Hastings. See you Monday.

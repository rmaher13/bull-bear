# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 27, 2026

*The market had the best month since forever, Big Tech reports this week, and oil could hit $95 because of a war nobody's talking about enough.*

THE OPEN

April is basically over and the S&P is up 9% for the month. The Nasdaq is up 15%. Your 401(k) had a better April than most people have had all year. Take a second. Appreciate it. Then brace yourself, because this week is the Super Bowl of earnings and the Fed meets Wednesday and there's a war pushing oil toward $95. It's fine. Probably fine.

BIG THREE

- Five of the Magnificent Seven report this week. Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, and Meta all drop Tuesday. Apple and Tesla follow later. The Magnificent Seven is just the nickname for the seven biggest tech companies — they're so large that when they sneeze, the whole market reaches for a tissue. The market has priced in great numbers from these guys. Meaning if they just do "good," Wall Street might throw a fit. It's the 180-bowling problem. We've talked about this. Good is only good enough if good is what everyone expected.

- Oil is at $95 a barrel and Goldman Sachs thinks it could average $90 through the end of the year — up from $80 just a few weeks ago. The Strait of Hormuz, that narrow little water highway in the Persian Gulf that roughly 20% of the world's oil has to squeeze through, is still messed up from the Iran conflict. Iran floated some kind of reopening proposal this weekend, and ceasefire talks are technically happening — except Trump just decided they'd do it by phone instead of in person, which is not exactly the vibe of guys who are close to a deal. Gas prices, heating costs, anything that gets shipped — it all runs on oil. This is the inflation story nobody's talking about enough.

- Jerome Powell is probably done as Fed chair. The DOJ dropped a criminal probe into him Friday, which freed up a Senate holdout to stop blocking Kevin Warsh's confirmation as the new chair. Powell likely presides over his last Fed meeting Wednesday — where they'll almost certainly do nothing with interest rates — and then hands the keys to Warsh in May. New sheriff, same interest rates, unclear vibes.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

Thursday, we get two huge economic reports on the same day. GDP and PCE. GDP — gross domestic product — is basically a report card for the whole economy. How much did America produce and sell last quarter? Estimate is 1.5% growth, up from a pretty weak 0.5% the quarter before. PCE is the Fed's favorite way to measure inflation — think of it like a monthly grocery receipt for the entire country. If either number comes in hot, meaning worse than expected, it changes how people think about interest rates and that moves markets fast. Two big swings in one day. Circle Thursday.

THE CLOSE

Best month in years, biggest earnings week of the year, new Fed chair incoming, and oil still doing whatever it wants. April went out swinging. Let's see if May can catch it.

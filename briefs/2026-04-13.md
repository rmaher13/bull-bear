**🧠 One Thing Worth Understanding**

**The Strait of Hormuz is basically the world's most important hallway.**

It's a narrow waterway between Iran and Oman — at its tightest point, only about 21 miles wide. Something like a fifth of all the world's traded oil flows through it. Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Kuwait, the UAE — most of their exports leave through this one skinny corridor. When it gets disrupted, even partially, oil prices spike fast because traders immediately price in the possibility of supply running short. It's not that we run out of oil tomorrow — it's that markets are always pricing in *what might happen next*, and a closed Hormuz is a very bad "what might happen next." The last time oil crossed $100 was 2022. We're back.

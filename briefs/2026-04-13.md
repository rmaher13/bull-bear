**🧠 One Thing Worth Understanding**

**The Strait of Hormuz — why one waterway can ruin your week.**

The Strait of Hormuz is a narrow channel between Iran and Oman, roughly 21 miles wide at its tightest point. About a fifth of the world's oil supply moves through it every single day — tankers carrying crude from Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Kuwait, and the UAE. When that lane gets disrupted, oil prices spike almost immediately, because the market doesn't wait to see how it plays out. Higher oil means higher gas. Higher gas means higher shipping costs. Higher shipping costs mean higher prices on basically everything that moves on a truck, which is everything. It's one of those places where the phrase "the global economy is connected" stops being an abstraction and starts showing up as a number on the gas pump.

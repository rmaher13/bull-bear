# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 19, 2026

*The Kelp DAO hack just ate $292 million and nobody at brunch mentioned it once.*

THE OPEN

Sunday morning. Markets are closed. Your 401(k) is sitting near all-time highs. And somewhere, a crypto hacker is having the best weekend of their life. We've got a lot of ground to cover.

BIG THREE

- The Strait of Hormuz story just keeps being the story. Iran opened it, oil cratered nearly 10%, stocks hit records, Bitcoin ticked up. We've been tracking this all week — last week Ken Griffin was saying a closed Strait meant global recession, and now oil is sitting under $95 and the market is throwing a ticker-tape parade. Brent crude is still at $95, which is elevated, but it's a long way from the $127 peak that was giving everyone ulcers. The war isn't over. The deal isn't signed. But the world's most important oil pipe is open again and markets are choosing optimism. Can't blame them.

- Netflix is still on the floor and hasn't gotten up. Down 9% after its earnings miss last week — not because revenue was bad, it wasn't, but because next quarter's forecast disappointed and Reed Hastings, the guy who literally started the whole thing, announced he's leaving the board in June. Wall Street treated a 16% revenue year like a participation trophy. The stock might bounce. Analysts say buy the dip. But right now Netflix is that running back who rushes for 110 yards and still gets benched because he fumbled at the goal line.

- The biggest DeFi hack of 2026 happened quietly this weekend and your group chat probably didn't mention it. Kelp DAO — a crypto platform — got drained of about $292 million overnight. DeFi stands for "decentralized finance," which basically means financial products running on code with no bank in the middle. Cool idea. Catastrophic when someone finds a hole in that code. The attacker walked out with 18% of the token's entire supply. Multiple platforms had to freeze operations. And Bitcoin and Ethereum both slipped on the news. This is the part of crypto that the "gonna make it" crowd glosses over. The technology is genuinely interesting. The security is genuinely not.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

The Fed is stuck and it's worth saying plainly one more time. The Fed controls interest rates — the lever that makes borrowing money more or less expensive for everyone. They want inflation at 2%. It's sitting at 3%. That gap means they can't lower rates without potentially making inflation worse. Now with oil still at $95 — well above pre-war levels — prices on stuff you actually buy aren't falling fast. The Fed's own people said this week they're in "wait and see" mode. Translation: your mortgage rate isn't dropping anytime soon. Your car loan either. The Strait being open helps. But it's not a cure.

THE CLOSE

Records on Wall Street. $292 million gone in DeFi. Oil falling. Rates frozen. It's Sunday. Touch some grass. We'll be back tomorrow.

# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 24, 2026

*Intel just rescued the Nasdaq from a software bloodbath, oil's at $107, and Meta fired 8,000 people to pay for a robot that writes emails.*

THE OPEN

The market tried to hit a record high yesterday, thought better of it, and backed away slowly like it knocked something over at someone else's house. Meanwhile, after the bell, Intel showed up and fixed the mood. Futures are green this morning. We're fine. Probably.

BIG THREE

- Intel popped 19% after hours. Nineteen. The chip market is already on a historic winning streak and now Intel — which has been the embarrassing cousin of the semiconductor family for about three years — beats on its sales forecast and goes absolutely feral. Nasdaq futures are up because of it. If chips keep doing this, your 401(k) is going to need new pants.

- Oil crossed $107 a barrel. The Strait of Hormuz — the little chokepoint in the Persian Gulf that something like 20% of the world's oil flows through — is still squeezed shut by the Iran conflict. Every barrel of crude that can't get through there pushes prices higher. Airlines are already warning you that tickets are going up. American Airlines cut its whole forecast for the year. Gas, flights, basically anything that moves — it's getting more expensive, and the Fed can't cut interest rates while inflation is running hot, so nothing's coming to save you on the mortgage either. Fun Friday.

- Meta is laying off 8,000 people on May 20th. Not to cut costs. To pay for AI. They're doing the thing where you fire a bunch of humans so you can buy a bunch of machines that might eventually do what the humans did. The people who got laid off probably aren't super psyched about being described as a "strategic reallocation." That's corporate for "we're betting on robots and you're not invited."

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

ServiceNow dropped almost 18% yesterday, which sounds like a company must have done something catastrophically wrong. They didn't. Their revenue grew — just not as fast as Wall Street was expecting. Here's the thing about earnings season: it's not about whether you made money. It's about whether you made MORE money than a bunch of analysts guessed you would. It's like if you bowled a 180 and everyone booed because they had you at 195. The score was fine. The vibe is everything. Software companies are getting hit hardest right now because the Middle East conflict is slowing down business deals overseas. Clients are distracted. Budgets are frozen. Nobody's signing a new software contract when the news is on fire.

KEEP AN EYE ON

- Intel gap-up open this morning could drag the whole Nasdaq higher. Watch whether it holds or fades by noon.
- Consumer sentiment number drops today. Last read was ugly. Economists expect slightly less ugly. Bar is low.

THE CLOSE

Oil at $107, chips going crazy, Meta handing out pink slips on a Tuesday in May. The market's trying to hit all-time highs through a war and a layoff wave and $5 gas. Honestly, respect the hustle. Have a good weekend.

# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 23, 2026

*Oil hit $104, chips just did something that's never happened before, and Tesla beat earnings — then got punished for it anyway.*

THE OPEN

Markets closed at all-time highs yesterday. Futures slipped this morning. Somewhere in between, oil crossed $104 a barrel, Tesla had a great quarter and got wrecked for it, and weed stocks ripped. Normal Wednesday.

BIG THREE

- Tesla beat on everything. Revenue, profit, margins — all better than expected. Stock dropped 3% anyway. Here's why: Elon got on the earnings call and told everyone the company is about to spend a ton more money on AI and robots. Wall Street heard "spend a ton more money" and did what Wall Street does, which is panic and sell. It's the GE Aerospace move from yesterday — great numbers, bad vibes, stock goes down. Tesla bulls are calling it a buying opportunity. Tesla bears are calling it a company that sells fewer cars every quarter. Both groups are very loud on the internet right now.

- Chipmakers just closed their 16th straight winning day. That has literally never happened before. Broadcom, AMD, Micron — all up 5 to 8% just yesterday. If you've got an index fund, which is basically just a basket holding a little bit of everything in the stock market, chips are a huge chunk of it. This is what's driving your 401(k) to all-time highs right now. Not glamorous, not exciting, just semiconductors going absolutely nuclear for over three weeks straight.

- Oil is at $104 a barrel and the IEA — basically the UN's energy guys — said we're facing the "biggest energy security threat in history." Casual. The Strait of Hormuz is still squeezed, Iran talks are still stalled, and crude is up 2% just today. This matters for everything. Oil feeds into gas prices, gas feeds into inflation, inflation is why the Fed still won't cut interest rates. The guys who control how much your mortgage costs are watching oil with the same energy as a guy checking fantasy scores on Sunday night.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

The FOMC meets on April 29th — that's the Federal Open Market Committee, which is the group of people who vote on whether to raise, lower, or hold interest rates. Right now, a prediction market called Polymarket has "no change" at 99%. Meaning almost nobody thinks they're moving. The Fed wants inflation down to 2%. We're sitting around 3.5%. With oil at $104, that gap isn't closing. So rates stay where they are, borrowing stays expensive, and your mortgage rate stays exactly as annoying as it is now. The meeting is mostly a formality at this point — but the press conference after is where things can get interesting.

KEEP AN EYE ON

- IBM dropped 6% after earnings despite beating — and quietly stopped reporting its AI revenue number. That's a weird thing to stop talking about.
- Cannabis stocks ripped on federal rescheduling news. Not legalization — just a tax break. Still worth watching if you're in that sector.

THE CLOSE

Record highs, $104 oil, and weed stocks had a better day than IBM. That's the market in April 2026. Get some rest.

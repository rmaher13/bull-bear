# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 25, 2026

*Chips hit all-time highs, Intel had its best day since Reagan, and regular people feel worse than they ever have. Cool cool cool.*

THE OPEN

Records on the S&P and Nasdaq again. Intel went absolutely insane. Nvidia crossed five trillion dollars. And consumer sentiment — how regular people actually feel about their lives — just hit an all-time low. So the market is doing great and America is miserable. Love the vibes. Very normal.

BIG THREE

- Intel was up 22% Friday. Twenty-two. That's the best single day for the stock since 1987, which means the last time Intel had a day this good, Top Gun was in theaters the first time. The chip sector is now on an 18-day winning streak — something that has literally never happened before in recorded market history. AMD up 13%, Qualcomm up 10%. If you've got a 401(k) or any kind of index fund, chips are a huge piece of it, and chips are cooking right now.

- Nvidia crossed five trillion dollars in market cap again. A trillion is a thousand billions. Five trillion is more than the entire economy of Japan. It's a number so stupid-large that your brain just rejects it and moves on, which is honestly the correct response. The wild part is it's sitting in most people's retirement accounts through index funds, whether they know it or not.

- Consumer sentiment just hit its worst level ever recorded. Worse than 2008. Worse than COVID. Regular people — not hedge fund guys, not CNBC anchors — are more anxious about their financial lives right now than at any point in the history of the survey. Meanwhile the stock market is at all-time highs. These two things being true at the same time is, to put it professionally, a little fucked up.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

There's a nuclear energy company called X-energy that went public Friday — meaning regular people can now buy stock in it for the first time. It raised over a billion dollars, the biggest nuclear IPO ever. Here's why this matters: AI needs an insane amount of electricity. Data centers running ChatGPT and everything like it are basically small cities in terms of power draw. Solar and wind can't scale fast enough. So suddenly nuclear, which everyone wrote off for thirty years, is back on the table. Advanced reactors, smaller designs, safer setups — money is flowing back into it hard. This isn't your dad's Three Mile Island situation. Worth at least knowing the story exists.

KEEP AN EYE ON

- Fed meets Tuesday and Wednesday. They're almost certainly holding interest rates where they are, but the press conference after is where surprises happen. Keep an ear out.
- Visa, Starbucks, Coca-Cola, UPS, T-Mobile all report earnings next week. Good chance at least one of those is sitting in your 401(k) right now.

THE CLOSE

Chips breaking records, Nvidia worth more than Japan, and a nuclear company just raised a billion dollars on its first day out. Meanwhile the rest of us are anxious as hell. Markets gonna market. Go enjoy your Saturday.

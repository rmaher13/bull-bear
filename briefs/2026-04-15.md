ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

The Fed chair is basically the most powerful unelected person in America, and we're getting a new one in a month. The Federal Reserve — the Fed — is the institution that sets interest rates, meaning it controls how expensive it is to borrow money. High rates? Your mortgage costs more. Your car payment costs more. Low rates? Cheaper to borrow, but inflation can run hot. Kevin Warsh is Trump's pick to run it, he's worth $135 million, and there's already a senator threatening to block his confirmation for reasons that have nothing to do with whether the guy can do the job. The confirmation hearing is April 21st. This actually matters to your mortgage rate and your savings account, which is more than I can say for most things happening in DC.

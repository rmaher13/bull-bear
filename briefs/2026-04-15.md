# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 15, 2026

*Banks are printing money, quantum stocks went insane, and there might be actual peace in Iran — but inflation's still being a dick.*

THE OPEN

Markets had a genuinely good day yesterday and Wall Street is walking around like a guy who just hit a parlay. S&P hit a new all-time high. Nasdaq had its best 11-day run on record. Even Bitcoin remembered it exists. The vibe is cautiously optimistic, which in market-speak means everyone's one bad headline away from panicking again.

BIG THREE

- JPMorgan made $16.5 billion in a single quarter. Morgan Stanley beat estimates by a mile. Bank earnings are reminding us that whatever chaos is happening in the world, the big banks are doing just fine, thank you. They're basically the house at the casino. The rest of us are the players.

- Quantum computing stocks went absolutely feral. IonQ up 21%. D-Wave up 22%. Rigetti up 13%. One day. For context, quantum computing is basically regular computing's weird genius cousin who's been in the garage for thirty years and might finally be about to do something incredible — or might just be loud about it. A government contract and a technical breakthrough sent the whole sector into orbit. If you don't own any and you're staring at this number with your mouth open, welcome to the club.

- The U.S.-Iran situation is calming down — and that's moving everything. Oil dropped from $104 a barrel at the peak of the conflict down to $91. Pakistan's army chief flew to Tehran to help extend the ceasefire. Trump says it's almost over. Peace talks moving forward = oil prices dropping = gas prices maybe getting less insane = inflation pressure easing. This is the domino everyone's watching right now.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

Inflation is the reason you feel broke even when nothing obviously bad happened to you. When inflation is high, everything costs more — groceries, rent, gas — and your paycheck buys less of it. The Fed's job is to keep inflation around 2% by adjusting interest rates. When inflation runs hot, they raise rates to slow things down. Right now inflation is at 3.3% — higher than they want — and the Fed has rates parked at 3.50-3.75% trying to squeeze it back down. The problem is those high rates make mortgages expensive, car loans expensive, credit card debt more brutal. So it's not great either way. The hope was that they'd cut rates in 2026. Now that's looking shakier. That matters for your 401(k), your car payment, and anyone trying to buy a house.

KEEP AN EYE ON

- Kevin Warsh's Fed Chair confirmation hearing hits the Senate on April 21. The next Fed chair will set interest rates for the next four years. Worth knowing his name.

THE CLOSE

Markets are up, banks are rich, quantum computers are having a moment, and there might be peace in the Middle East. Somehow inflation is still the buzzkill at the party. Classic inflation.

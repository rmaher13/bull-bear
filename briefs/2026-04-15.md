**🧠 One Thing Worth Understanding**

**"Sell the news" — what it actually means.**

Goldman Sachs posted near-record profits this morning, and its stock *went down*. That sounds insane. It's not. It's a thing called "sell the news," and here's the deal: by the time a company actually reports great earnings, traders have already been betting on great earnings for weeks. The price goes up *in anticipation*. So when the good news finally arrives, there's nothing left to buy — and a lot of people who already profited decide it's a good time to cash out. The result: the news is great, the stock drops, and you feel like you're losing your mind. You're not. Wall Street just has a weird way of celebrating.

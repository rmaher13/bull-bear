# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 15, 2026

*Stocks are knocking on the door of an all-time high and the Fed just crossed its arms and said "nope."*

THE OPEN

Markets are up, oil is down, your 401(k) is almost back to its peak, and somehow the most exciting thing on the board today is banks reporting earnings. We live in interesting times.

BIG THREE

- The S&P 500 is sitting at 6,967 — basically touching an all-time high — because traders got excited about possible peace talks in the Middle East. Oil dropped 7% in a single day on the news. That's a big deal. When oil gets cheaper, everything that runs on or gets shipped by oil gets cheaper too. Your gas bill, your groceries, your Amazon boxes. Peace isn't confirmed. But markets bet on rumors and pray for news.

- Bank earnings were a mixed bag and Wall Street handled it the way guys handle bad news at the bar — shrugged and ordered another round. JPMorgan made more money, then warned things might get complicated. Wells Fargo beat expectations and somehow still dropped 5%. Finance is deeply stupid sometimes. Today, Bank of America and Morgan Stanley step up to the plate.

- Goldman Sachs filed to launch a Bitcoin ETF — basically a fund where you can invest in Bitcoin without actually buying Bitcoin, through a regular brokerage account like the one your 401(k) is in. This is Wall Street deciding crypto is no longer a weird cousin at Thanksgiving. It's now sitting at the main table, eating the good stuff.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

Inflation is still running at 3.3% — meaning prices overall are about 3.3% higher than they were a year ago. That sounds abstract until you hear that nearly half of that is just gas. The war in the Middle East spiked oil, oil spiked gas, gas dragged everything else up with it. The Fed — the folks who set interest rates, which control how expensive it is to borrow money for a house, a car, a credit card — their whole job right now is to get inflation back down to 2%. But they can't really fix a war. So they're just sitting there, holding rates where they are, waiting. Rates stay high. Your mortgage stays expensive. That's the deal until something changes.

KEEP AN EYE ON

- Bank of America and Morgan Stanley report this morning. If they both look healthy, that's a good sign the economy is hanging in there despite everything.
- April 28-29 is the Fed meeting. Nobody expects them to move rates — the market is pricing it at 97% odds they hold. But what they *say* about the future matters as much as what they do.

THE CLOSE

The stock market is basically one piece of peace-deal news away from a record high. Which is a wild sentence, but here we are. Go check your 401(k). It's probably having a better week than you are.

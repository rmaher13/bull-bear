**🧠 One Thing Worth Understanding**

**Why does oil dropping matter so much to everything else?**

When oil gets expensive, it costs more to make stuff, ship stuff, and heat your house — which pushes inflation up. When inflation goes up, the Fed raises rates to fight it, which makes borrowing more expensive for everyone: businesses, homebuyers, people buying cars. Higher rates also make "safe" investments like bonds more attractive, so money moves out of stocks and crypto and into bonds. It's a whole domino chain that starts at the gas pump. Right now, oil briefly hit $100 a barrel during the worst of the Iran conflict. It's back at $96 today and falling. That's still not cheap, but the direction matters. Markets aren't reacting to where oil is — they're reacting to where it's going.

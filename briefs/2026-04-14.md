**🧠 One Thing Worth Understanding**

**What a "de minimis" exemption is — and why it matters if you own any crypto.**

Right now, every time you use crypto to buy something — even a $12 sandwich — it's technically a taxable event. You have to track what you paid for that Bitcoin, what it was worth when you spent it, and report any gain. It is exactly as annoying as it sounds. The PARITY Act, re-introduced in Congress this week, would create a "de minimis" exemption — basically a threshold below which small crypto purchases wouldn't trigger a tax-reporting requirement. Think of it like how you don't report the $3 in interest your savings account paid in 2019. It doesn't mean crypto gains disappear, it just means using crypto for small everyday purchases wouldn't bury you in paperwork. It hasn't passed yet, and Congress moves at the speed of a confused glacier, but it's worth watching if you hold any crypto at all.

**🧠 One Thing Worth Understanding**

**Why do bank stocks fall when earnings are good?**

Here's the thing nobody explains: a stock price isn't about what happened — it's about what people *expected* to happen. If everyone already figured Goldman was gonna crush it, that expectation gets baked into the stock price *before* earnings drop. Then the report comes out, it's great, and there's nothing left to get excited about. Traders call it "buy the rumor, sell the news." It's like hyping a movie for two years — by opening night, you've already kind of seen it. Goldman had a monster quarter and the stock still slipped because the bar was already sky-high. Doesn't mean the company is struggling. It just means Wall Street already priced in the win.

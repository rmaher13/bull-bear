# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 14, 2026

*The market almost forgot there's a war on.*

THE OPEN

Green across the board, earnings coming in strong, and your 401(k) is almost back to where it was before everything went sideways. Good morning. Don't get too comfortable.

BIG THREE

- The S&P 500 is now less than one percent from its all-time high. To put that in perspective: a few weeks ago we were watching it drop like a check engine light came on. Now it's basically recovered. The Nasdaq has gone up ten days in a row — its best streak in five years. Tech carried the whole thing, which means if you've got a 401(k) sitting in any kind of growth fund, you've been quietly having a good week whether you knew it or not.

- JPMorgan crushed earnings. Goldman Sachs had their second-best quarter ever. Then Goldman's stock went DOWN. That's Wall Street doing Wall Street things — "good news already priced in" is the finance version of "yeah, but." The bank put up historic numbers and traders shrugged like a guy who just got a raise but wanted a bigger one. Exhausting people.

- CarMax dropped 15% in one day. CarMax is the big used car dealership chain, and their numbers were rough compared to last year. Worth paying attention to if you're thinking about buying or selling a car soon, because what hurts CarMax usually means the used car market is softening. Could be good news for buyers. Just noting it.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

Inflation came in at 3.3% last month. Here's what that means in plain English: a thing that cost $100 a year ago now costs $103.30. The main culprit is gas prices — energy swings around a lot, which is why economists also look at "core inflation," which just strips out food and gas to get a cleaner read. Core came in at 2.6%. The Fed — the guys who set interest rates for the whole country — want inflation closer to 2% before they'll cut rates. Cutting rates is basically the Fed making it cheaper to borrow money, which tends to get the economy moving. At 3.3%, they're not doing that yet. There's a 97% chance they hold steady at their meeting on April 28th. So don't hold your breath for cheaper mortgages or car loans anytime soon.

KEEP AN EYE ON

- The Strait of Hormuz situation. That's the narrow waterway where a huge chunk of the world's oil moves through. The U.S. blockaded Iranian oil shipments there. One Wall Street CEO said if it stays shut for six to twelve months, the whole world goes into a recession. Iran apparently called Washington. Something's happening. We just don't know what yet.

- Jerome Powell is out as Fed chair on May 15th. His replacement has crypto in his personal portfolio. Make of that what you will.

THE CLOSE

Your retirement account recovered. Inflation is still annoying. There's a naval blockade happening. It's a lot for a Tuesday. Go touch grass.

# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 26, 2026

*Records again, chips again, Meta fired 8,000 people, and cable TV is officially dying in real time.*

THE OPEN

S&P and Nasdaq closed at all-time highs again Friday. At some point we just have to accept that "all-time high" is the new normal and stop acting surprised, like finding out your buddy's been winning at poker every week. Eventually you stop celebrating and start wondering what the hell is going on.

BIG THREE

- Intel went up 20%, dragged AMD up 12%, Nvidia crossed five trillion again, and the chip sector is still doing things that have never happened in market history. We covered this Thursday. We covered it Friday. We'll cover it again because it's still happening. The CPU — the main processor chip in basically every computer that exists — is apparently "reinserting itself as the indispensable foundation of the AI era." That's a direct quote from an analyst, and yes, it sounds like something a guy writes when he needs to justify his salary. But the numbers are real. Chips are carrying this entire market on their back right now.

- Meta confirmed 8,000 layoffs starting May 20th. Eight thousand people. And they're also canceling 6,000 open jobs that hadn't been filled yet. The reason? AI investment. They're cutting humans to fund machines that might eventually do what the humans did. The crazy part is Meta stock is fine. Wall Street loves this move. "Efficient" is the word they use. The 8,000 people who are about to get a calendar invite from HR probably have a different word.

- Charter Communications fell 23% in a single day after losing 120,000 cable internet subscribers in one quarter. Twenty-three percent. In one day. That's not a stumble, that's a crater. Comcast got downgraded and fell 8% the same day. Cable companies are hemorrhaging customers and there's no obvious way to stop it. People are cutting the cord, switching to fiber, using their phones. The cable bundle that used to print money for thirty years is coming apart at the seams and Friday was a very ugly reminder of that.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

Earnings season is running hot right now. "Earnings" just means: companies reporting how much money they made last quarter. Analysts guess a number ahead of time, companies report the real number, and the market reacts to the gap between those two. Right now, the average S&P 500 company is beating those guesses by a mile — earnings growth is tracking at 16%, up from 14% just a few weeks ago. That's a big jump. And it's one of the main reasons the market keeps hitting records even though oil is near $100, inflation is still above where the Fed wants it, and regular people feel like garbage. The companies are doing fine. The vibe is just bad. Both things are true at the same time.

THE CLOSE

Chips on a historic streak, Meta cutting bodies to buy bots, and cable TV losing the will to live. The market's at records and none of us feel like it. Sunday. Go outside.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

The Strait of Hormuz is a waterway about 21 miles wide at its narrowest point — roughly the distance from downtown Boston to the airport. About 20 million barrels of oil pass through it every single day. That's roughly 20% of the world's oil supply squeezing through one skinny channel between Iran and Oman. When it's open, oil flows, prices stabilize, your gas bill is manageable. When Iran shuts it down — or even threatens to — oil traders panic, prices spike, and it shows up at the pump within days. It's the world's most consequential bottleneck. One country with its hand on that faucet has more influence over your Monday morning than most things you actually vote on.

# Bull & Bear With Me — Apr 21, 2026

*Oil's at $89, the next Fed chair is on Capitol Hill auditioning for a job he might not actually have power to do, and Apple just fired its most famous employee.*

THE OPEN

Yesterday was the pullback after the pullback after the record high. Small dip, nothing dramatic. Markets are doing that thing where they're not sure whether to be scared or fine, so they're just kind of standing there in the parking lot, keys in hand, not going anywhere.

BIG THREE

- Oil is the whole story and it keeps being the whole story. The Strait of Hormuz is still being squeezed, a ceasefire deadline hits this week, and crude jumped to $89 a barrel — that's the market pricing in "this probably gets worse before it gets better." We were celebrating $89 as a relief price like two weeks ago. Now it's the scary number. Gas is $4.11 nationally. Your household is paying roughly $190 more a month in energy costs than before any of this started. That's a car payment for some people. Real money.

- The next Fed chair nominee — a guy named Kevin Warsh — spent Tuesday on Capitol Hill trying to convince lawmakers he'll lower interest rates while also being totally independent and not just doing whatever DC tells him to. Those two things are kind of in conflict! The Fed — which is basically the committee that decides how expensive it is to borrow money for a mortgage, a car, a credit card — sets rates by committee vote, not by one guy's opinion. And right now most of that committee isn't cutting anything because inflation is running at 3.58% when they want it at 2%. The Iran war made that worse. So Warsh may get the job and then immediately not be able to do the main thing he was hired to do. Welcome to Washington.

- UnitedHealth crushed earnings and popped over 6%. If you've got a broad index fund — basically a basket of stocks that tracks the whole market — UNH is in there, and it helped offset an otherwise flat day. Apple, on the other hand, named a new CEO and dropped. John Ternus is taking over from Tim Cook, which is genuinely huge news, and the stock slipped about half a percent after hours as people processed it. Apple is one of the biggest weights in almost every retirement account in America. Worth watching how the market fully digests it today.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

Inflation and interest rates are a seesaw. When prices rise too fast — groceries, gas, rent — the Fed raises rates to cool things down. Higher rates make borrowing more expensive, so people spend less, demand drops, prices stabilize. The problem right now is oil is keeping inflation elevated, which means the Fed can't lower rates even though a lot of people really want them to. Every dollar more you're paying at the pump is another reason your mortgage rate stays high. The Strait of Hormuz isn't just a gas problem. It's a everything-costs-money problem.

THE CLOSE

Tesla reports tomorrow. Apple just changed CEOs. Oil is still oil. Get some sleep.

Think of it like a fantasy football team where one guy is going off for 50 points and everyone else is averaging six. The index looks great because the biggest tech names are carrying it. Microsoft, Amazon, Meta — those guys report tonight and tomorrow. If they disappoint, this whole record-setting run gets tested real fast. The party is real. The guest list is just smaller than it looks.

- Oil hit $99.77 a barrel on the way to $100, and it's the Iran situation we talked about yesterday — the Strait of Hormuz is still a mess, peace talks are going nowhere, and Goldman Sachs just bumped their year-end forecast up. We've covered this all week. The short version: $100 oil means higher gas prices, higher shipping costs, and higher prices on basically anything that gets driven or flown anywhere. That's inflation. And inflation is exactly why the Fed is doing nothing tomorrow.

ONE THING WORTH UNDERSTANDING

The Fed "holds rates" — you're going to hear that phrase tomorrow and it's worth actually knowing what it means. The Fed controls the interest rate that banks charge each other to borrow money overnight. When that rate goes up, everything gets more expensive to borrow — mortgages, car loans, credit cards, all of it. When it goes down, borrowing gets cheaper and people spend more. Right now, inflation is still a little high and oil is threatening to push it higher. So the Fed is stuck. Cut rates and inflation gets worse. Raise them and you choke the economy. So they do nothing. "Holding rates" is the Fed equivalent of deciding not to make a move in chess because every option looks bad. They'll announce it tomorrow afternoon. Nobody expects a surprise.
